This is super basic, but I wanted to include it.

I’m a serial eat-outer, and would sometimes combine that with my habit of taking walks in the neighborhood at odd hours. I wanted a comprehensive idea of my options.

I remember looking up restaurants’ websites, putting their hours in a spreadsheet, and then counting out cells in my notebook with a pencil. It’s the first thing I ever tried after my boss let me borrow his copies of Edward Tufte’s books, and I got into all this.

Food spots are ordered roughly by their distance from my apartment at the time. In this way, it’s personalized, like an analog, frozen “Food Near Me” maps search.

Dessert

Charting out the rest of the week would be a perfect use case for a small multiple.

It could be fun to find an API to dynamically pull hours from restaurants’ online listings, in case hours change, or just for the principle of dynamic data drawing.

I could also use a current location API to display restaurants within some set walkable distance for the viewer’s current location.

But at this point I’d just be reinventing the Google Maps “Explore feature.“ For now, I think I like it better as a manual exercise. (Did I mention I drew this in a booth at Yesterdog? I could’ve been at Pita House the whole time.)
